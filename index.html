Conjugation

Tagadnes forma · Groups 1 & 2 + Reflexives
Latvian verbs fall into conjugation groups. Group 1 infinitives end in -āt and retain the vowel ā throughout the present. Group 2 infinitives end in -īt or -ināt — the vowel is dropped and endings attach to the bare stem. Reflexive verbs follow the same groups but use -os/-ies/-ās/-āmies/-āties/-ās throughout.

Notes on Group 1

  • All Group 1 verbs end in -āt. The long vowel ā is visible in every present tense form.
  • The es-form inserts -āj- before the ending: stem + āju.
  • tu, viņš/viņa, and viņi/viņas are all identical — bare stem + ā. Pronoun or context identifies the person.
  • mēs and jūs also use -āj-: stem + ājam / ājat.

Notes on Reflexive Verbs

  • Endings: -os, -ies, -ās, -āmies, -āties, -ās — these replace the standard endings entirely.
  • 3rd person singular and plural always share the same form: -ās.
  • Group 1 reflexives keep -āj- connector (e.g. mazgāj-os). Group 2 attach directly to the stem (e.g. māc-os).
  • Some reflexives have no non-reflexive counterpart (smieties, celties). Others are reflexive versions of transitives: mazgāt → mazgāties.

Key Notes

  • Group 1: ā present in every form. tu/viņš/viņa/viņi/viņas all end in bare -ā; es/mēs/jūs use -āj-.
  • Group 2: infinitive vowel dropped completely. Endings -u/-i/-a/-ām/-āt/-a on bare stem.
  • Reflexives: Group 1 keeps -āj- before ending; Group 2 attaches directly to stem.
  • Present tense covers both "I read" and "I am reading" — no separate continuous form.

Настоящее время · Both conjugation classes
Russian verbs fall into two conjugation classes. The 1st conjugation (е-conjugation) uses е/ё in endings — most -ать verbs. The 2nd conjugation (и-conjugation) uses и — most -ить verbs. In the 1st conjugation, a mutating stem consonant changes in every form. In the 2nd conjugation, mutations only occur in the я-form. Consonant Mutations

Чередование согласных · behaviour differs between conjugation classes

In 1st conjugation (-ать) verbs, a mutating stem consonant changes in every present form. In 2nd conjugation (-ить) verbs, the mutation occurs only in the я-form — all other forms keep the original stem.

Key Rules

  • 1st conjugation: mutations apply to every person — пишу, пишешь, пишет, пишем, пишете, пишут.
  • 2nd conjugation: mutations apply to the я-form only — люблю, but любишь, любит, любим…
  • The same consonant can mutate in both conjugations (e.g. с→ш, з→ж) but the scope differs.
  • Labial mutations (б, в, п, м, ф) insert л — they only occur in the 2nd conjugation.
