Conjugation

Настоящее время · Both conjugation classes
Russian verbs fall into two conjugation classes. The 1st conjugation (е-conjugation) uses the vowel е/ё in its endings and covers most -ать verbs. The 2nd conjugation (и-conjugation) uses и and covers most -ить verbs plus a small set of -еть/-ать exceptions. In the 2nd conjugation, the stem's final consonant often mutates in the я-form only (е.g. в→вл, д→ж, т→ч). Reflexive verbs add -сь after vowel endings (я, вы) and -ся after all other endings.

Notes on the 1st Conjugation

  • Most -ать verbs belong here. The vowel е (or ё when stressed) appears in all endings except я and они.
  • In the я-form, the ending is -ю (after a vowel stem) or -у (after a consonant or hushing consonant).
  • In the они-form, the ending is -ют (after a vowel stem) or -ут (after a consonant).
  • When a stem ends in a mutating consonant (с, з, к, г, ск…), the mutation applies to every person — unlike the 2nd conjugation where only я mutates.
  • When the ending is stressed, е becomes ё: e.g. жить → живёшь, живёт…

Notes on the 2nd Conjugation

  • Most -ить verbs belong here. The vowel и appears in all endings except я and они.
  • In the я-form only, the stem-final consonant often mutates. All other forms use the unchanged stem.
  • Labial consonants (б, в, п, м, ф) insert an л: любить → люблю, готовить → готовлю.
  • Other mutations: д/з→ж, с→ш, ст→щ, т→ч. Only applies to the я-form.
  • Several common -еть and -ать verbs also follow the 2nd conjugation, e.g. смотреть (to watch), слышать (to hear).
  • In the они-form, the ending is -ят (most verbs) or -ат after hushing consonants (ж, ч, ш, щ).

Reflexive Verbs

Возвратные глаголы · Infinitive ends in -ться / -иться / -аться

Conjugate a reflexive verb exactly like its non-reflexive counterpart, then add the reflexive particle: -сь after a vowel ending (я → …сь, вы → …сь), and -ся after a consonant ending (all other forms). Both are reduced from the old reflexive pronoun себя.

Notes on Reflexive Verbs

  • Add -сь after a vowel-ending form (я and вы), and -ся after a consonant-ending form (ты, он, мы, они).
  • The underlying conjugation class (1st or 2nd) is the same as the non-reflexive base verb.
  • Consonant mutations still apply in the я-form for 2nd conjugation reflexives (e.g. нравиться → я нравлюсь).
  • Many reflexive verbs have no non-reflexive counterpart (e.g. бояться, надеяться). Others are reflexive versions of transitive verbs (учить → учиться).
  • Note that -тся (he/she/it, they) and -ться (infinitive) are pronounced identically as [цa] — a common source of spelling errors.

Consonant Mutations

Чередование согласных · Behaviour differs between conjugation classes

Consonant mutations work differently depending on conjugation class. In 1st conjugation (-ать) verbs, if the stem ends in a mutating consonant, it changes in every present tense form — я, ты, он, мы, вы, они all use the mutated stem. In 2nd conjugation (-ить) verbs, the mutation occurs only in the я-form — all other forms keep the original stem consonant.

Key Rules

  • In the 1st conjugation (-ать verbs), if the stem ends in a mutating consonant, it mutates in every present tense form: писать → пишу, пишешь, пишет, пишем, пишете, пишут.
  • In the 2nd conjugation (-ить verbs), the mutation occurs in the я-form only. All other forms keep the original stem: любить → люблю, но любишь, любит, любим…
  • Labial mutations (б, в, п, м, ф) insert л rather than replacing the consonant — this only occurs in 2nd conjugation.
  • The same consonant (e.g. с→ш, з→ж) can appear in both conjugations, but the scope of mutation differs: all forms in 1st, я only in 2nd.
  • These mutations arise from historical palatalisation — the ending -ю/-у originally contained a palatal glide that softened the preceding consonant.

Key Grammar Notes

  • 1st conjugation (е-conjugation): uses е/ё in all endings except я (-ю/-у) and они (-ют/-ут). Use -ё when the ending is stressed.
  • 2nd conjugation (и-conjugation): uses и in all endings except я (-ю/-у) and они (-ят/-ат after most consonants, -ат after ж/ч/ш/щ).
  • Both conjugations share the same я ending (-ю after vowels, -у after consonants or hushing consonants ж/ч/ш/щ).
  • Russian present tense is only used for imperfective verbs. Perfective verbs use the same endings but to express future tense.
  • Subject pronouns are often omitted since the verb ending already identifies the person.
